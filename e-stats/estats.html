Rise of E-commerce in this pandemic







Why did E-commerce prosper more in the pandemic than regular shopping

Since the dawn of this pandemic,e-commerce played an immensily significant role in people's life. Since they couldn't reach out to the regular stores for supplies,e-commerce platforms such as Amazon were the only way to get resources such as groceries

Why people hesitated in the beginning to buys things from e-commerce platforms

This was so because of the fact that when the pandemic wasn't in existence, the quality of the products were much poorer and the prices were too high for the products, But as of now there is no choice but to trust and buy goods from the e-commerce platforms

How did E-commerce help in stabalizing the downfall of global economy

After the pandemic started started the Global economy was at its downfall, the market started going down at an alarming rate most people accepted that after the pandemic the economy will drop to a rate that was never considered even to be imagined. But with people using e-commerce platforms the rate of the global market is stabalizing and hopefully would get to its average point.

Will E-commerce platforms takeover the market industry after the pandemic ends?

Yes and no. People have started to realize that ordering things online is a lot convinient since you just have to press a few buttons and done, as simple as that. It will influence a lot of people to switch from regular shopping to online shopping. But there a few downfalls which may restrict people to use e-commerce platforms as their core source of items. online shopping takes a lot of time shipping whereas regular shopping is way more effective in terms of time comsumption. There more drawbacks like quality, difference in prices etc.

About Some E-commerce platforms

Amazon

Amazon is of the leading E-commerce company to emerge. According to data records, the AMZN stock rised by 157% in just 6 months in the pandemic. Amazon is the biggest E-commerce company as of now, leaving behind Flipkart and BigBasket out of the league. The CEO, Jeff Bezos, became the richest man in the world with the net worth of $193 billion. He earns $3,715 per second. This was all thanks to the pandemic that Amazon became the biggest company in the world. They also launched many other products such as AWS , Amazon Pay etc.

Big Basket

Big Basket is yet another e-commerce platform to buy goods and groceries for household. It has made a big impact in India because of the fact that groceries were on top of the list for resources and it was perfect since it provided all the essential items needed. Although it didn't make much of an impact in the foreign lands but did in India. They also have plans to put up medicines in the store.

Amazon's technology to let shoppers pay with a 'palm-scan'

Amazon.com Inc said it is rolling out biometric technology at its Whole Foods stores around Seattle starting on Wednesday, letting shoppers pay for items with a scan of their palm. The move shows how Amazon is bringing some of the technology already in use at its namesake brick-and-mortar Go and Books stores to the grocery chain it acquired in 2017. The system, called Amazon One, lets customers associate a credit card with their palm print. It offers a contact-less alternative to cash and card payments, Amazon said.




Flipkart expands 90-min delivery service Quick to six new cities

Flipkart has expanded its hyperlocal delivery service Quick to six new cities including Delhi, Gurugram, Ghaziabad, Noida, Hyderabad and Pune as the demand for essential goods on e-commerce platforms surges amid the second wave of the pandemic. The Walmart-owned company, which had launched the service in Bengaluru last year, said customers will be able to get items such as fresh fruits and vegetables, dairy, meat, groceries, mobiles, electronics and baby-care products delivered in under 90 minutes or between pre-selected two-hour slots. Flipkart added that it would roll out the service in other metros and smaller cities in phases this year. Flipkart Quick will offer free deliveries on orders above Rs 499 between 6 am and midnight.




Amazon says it registered 5 million merchants on its payments platform

Amazon on Saturday said it has registered 5 million merchants through Amazon Pay - its payments unit - and that it remains focussed on helping more small and medium businesses (SMBs) in the country to embrace digital payments. These merchants, many of whom earlier transacted only in cash, are now accepting payments from their customers using Amazon Pay's QR Code. "Since 2019, we have registered 5 million merchants through Amazon Pay and gotten 40 million customers through our service to use UPI. We're obviously not alone and the more companies like Amazon, and our competitors all help, the flywheel spins faster," Amazon Senior Vice President Russell Grandinetti said during the Amazon Smbhav event. He added organisations and governments across the world are not only inspired by Aadhaar, the India Stack and UPI but they are also looking at emulating these models.
